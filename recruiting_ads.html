Note 1:

Due to the high volume of emails, I will NOT respond to generic inquiries lacking specific details (i.e., an email could be sent to any professor).

I am willing to reply if (1) I find your background particularly relevant and (2) you follow the instructions below.

Ph.D. Students

I am always on the lookout for motivated Ph.D. students with strong research interests in security, machine learning, computer architecture, high-efficiency computing, and the Internet of Things (IoT). If you’re interested in exploring these fields with me, please:

  • First, apply to the Ph.D. program in the ECE department (Computer Engineering or CE).

  • Second, mention my name as your preferred advisor in your application materials (e.g., in your statement of purpose) for easy identification.

Feel free to email me, but please ensure that:

  • Step 1: Use the subject line Prospective PhD Student - Semester/Year (e.g., Fall 2027). This helps prevent your email from being marked as spam.

  • Step 2: Share insights from at least one of my recent papers, noting which paper interests you and why. Again, I will not respond to generic applications without such details.

Postdocs

We have postdoctoral openings available. If you’re interested in joining my research, please send your CV along with 1-2 of your representative papers.

MS Students at NEU

If you’d like to conduct research with me, a good approach is to (1) take my class or (2) read a couple of my recent papers and share your insights.

Undergraduate at NEU

If you’re interested in working with me, a great first step is to enroll in my course or review my recent papers and discuss your findings with me.
